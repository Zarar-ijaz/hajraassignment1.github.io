About Creativity


Teachers can create an environment where children can express themselves with confidence and curiosity. Teachers can ask children open-ended questions to encourage them to express themselves and find their own answers.

Teachers can share their own creative interests, questions, and experiences with children. Teachers can demonstrate interest and excitement when working with children. Creativity


The need for the teachers of one of the private universities which is located within the Eastern Mediterranean region and is in charge of the Department of Education not only to recall their understanding of creativity but also to examine the assumptions and perceptions of creativity among early childhood student teachers arose from the pandemic teaching and learning arrangement. More specifically, we investigated the beliefs regarding the creative ideals, traits of the creative person, and the nature and potential of the creative education process and its importance for the professional improvement of 15 junior early childhood teachers students through informal interviews with these early childhood students. The article addresses the outcome of a qualitative descriptive research investigation carried out in the context of the course entitled Designing Activities in Kindergarten. Furthermore, it addresses the findings of the semi-structured interviews which aimed at finding out what concepts regarding creativity early childhood students have, and what evidence they need to support the idea that early childhood students need a structure and a generic scheme for idea and lesson development.

While creativity was reserved for the elite during the Renaissance period, Csikszentmihaly (2006) acknowledges how far along the evolution of our species has come where now creativity has become a necessity for us all. According to De Bono (1992), “Creativity is priceless; its importance is indisputable. It is development at its core. For there is no creativity (and thus no inspiration, p. 169) without creativity, and if there is no creativity life would go the same way ad infinitum” (p. 168).
